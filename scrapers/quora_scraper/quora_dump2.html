    Kim Scheinberg 2 votes (show)Loading...
    I don't have a long Quora history, but I'll throw out a theory:

    The high volume answerers result in a high volume feed. It took me a while to commit to following J.C. Hewitt -- not because he doesn't write great answers, but because he writes a lot of them.

    I still haven't been able to steel myself to follow Michael Wolfe, though I expend extra effort to find his answers, and he has 3 (possibly 4) of my Top 10 favorite Quora posts. (And I have to swallow so much envy reading his stuff.)

    And I regularly contemplate unfollowing Marc Bodnick -- not because his answers aren't fantastic, but because too many of them skew toward subjects that I wouldn't read about even if Shakespeare's ghost were writing the answers. (Plus, you know, the guy's a little profligate with the upvotes.)

    For me, it's about the fire hose.
